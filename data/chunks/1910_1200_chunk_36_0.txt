The classification of a substance as a Category 1 carcinogen is done on the basis of epidemiological and/or animal data. This classification is further distinguished on the basis of whether the evidence for classification is largely from human data (Category 1A) or from animal data (Category 1B): Category 1A: Known to have carcinogenic potential for humans. Classification in this category is largely based on human evidence. Category 1B: Presumed to have carcinogenic potential for humans. Classification in this category is largely based on animal evidence.
